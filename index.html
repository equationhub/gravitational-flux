Gauss's Law for Gravitational Flux

\( \Phi_g = \displaystyle\oint \vec{g} \cdot d\vec{A} = -4\pi GM_{\text{encl}}\)


In physics, Gauss's Law for Gravitational Flux or, Gravity, also known as Gauss's Flux Theorem for Gravity, is a law of physics that is equivalent to Newton's law of universal gravitation. It is named after Carl Friedrich Gauss. Gauss's Law for Gravitational Flux is often more convenient to work from than Newton's Law. The form of Gauss's Law for Gravitational Flux is mathematically similar to Gauss's Law for Electric Flux, one of Maxwell's equations. Gauss's Law for Gravitational Flux has the same mathematical relation to Newton's law that Gauss's Law for Electric Flux bears to Coulomb's law. This is because both Newton's law and Coulomb's law describe inverse-square interaction in a 3-dimensional space.
